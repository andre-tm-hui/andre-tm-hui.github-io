VR Drum Simulator

A virtual reality drumming simulator, designed to be as true-to-life as possible, with responsive interactions. This simulator includes:

  • Assortment of kit parts: Snare, Kick, Hi-Hat, 2 Crashes, 3 Toms and a Ride Cymbal, all modelled in Blender
  • Specific interactions including Rimshots on the snare, an alternate ride tone based on hit position and the ability to choke cymbals
  • Kit I/O: save and load different kit setups easily
  • Play your own music: play audio files in the game by placing audio files in a designated folder
  • MIDI I/O: connect the virtual kit to your DAW/plugin of choice (requires loopMIDI for Windows)
  • WIP: Analog kick and hi-hat pedal interactions, allowing control over their dynamics

Future Additions:

  • MIDI follow-along: load a MIDI file corresponding to a song, and play along using visual indicators
  • Performance scoring: compare your own performance to a MIDI file to gauge your accuracy

Challenge Log:

  • Collision causing multiple audio events: solved by setting a timeout equivalent to the world-record for fastest one-handed drum roll.
  • Missed collisions due to drumsticks passing through the entire collider in a single frame: solved by interpolating the drumstick's path and using raycasts to check for collisions.
  • Single drum strokes 'hitting through' multiple drums: only process the first raycast collision point found (see above).
  • No native support for MIDI output: used JUCE framework to develop a Unity Native Plugin that outputs to a Virtual MIDI Device.
  • Reducing unintentional strikes: find the normal to the top surface of a drum/cymbal, and compare the velocity of the stick to the normal. If the angle between the two vectors is less than a defined threshold, initiate an audio event.

VR Piano Simulator

A virtual reality piano/keyboard simulator, in very early development, which:

  • Maps a piano to a flat surface: place your hands on a flat surface for 3 seconds to spawn a piano on that surface. This solves the lack of tactile feedback as found in other implementations.
  • Uses hand-tracking: currently using Oculus hand-tracking, but I might swap to LucasVRTech's VR Gloves to improve accuracy.
  • Implements a sustain pedal by either: using head motion/pose or external input devices.
  • Outputs MIDI with loopMIDI on Windows.

Current Challenges:

  • Quest Hand-Tracking: introduces noticeable latency in collisions, and not supported in PCVR, hence making it impossible to output MIDI.
